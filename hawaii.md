# Hawaii

Hawaii is a beautiful archipelago in the middle of the Pacific Ocean, made up of eight main islands, each with its own personality, climate, and landscape. From the surf breaks of Oahu to the dramatic cliffs of Kauai, Hawaii offers a rare mix of lush rainforest, volcanic terrain, and postcard-perfect beaches all within a few hours' flight of each other. The islands are also home to Native Hawaiian culture, language, and traditions that shape everything from the food to the music to the spirit of aloha that visitors feel the moment they step off the plane. I plan to visit soon, and Hawaii is at the top of my travel list for its natural beauty and laid-back pace of life.

![Hawaii coastline with palm trees and turquoise water](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?hawaii,pacific-ocean)

In particular, I hope to visit Kona, on the west side of the Big Island (Hawaii Island). Kona is famous for its sunny, dry weather, calm waters, and dramatic volcanic coastline, a big contrast to the rainier windward side of the island. It's also world-renowned for Kona coffee, grown on the slopes of Hualalai and Mauna Loa in the "Kona Coffee Belt," where volcanic soil and misty afternoons create ideal growing conditions. Kailua-Kona, the main town, has a charming waterfront with historic sites, shops, and restaurants, making it a great home base for exploring the rest of the island.

![Kailua-Kona coastline on the Big Island](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?kona,hawaii,coastline)

## Things to Do & Sights

- **Snorkel or dive at Kealakekua Bay** – a marine sanctuary with incredible visibility, coral reefs, and spinner dolphins, plus the historic Captain Cook Monument.

  ![Snorkeling over a coral reef](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?snorkeling,coral-reef)

- **Watch the sunset at Magic Sands Beach** – a small, popular beach in Kona known for shifting sand and gorgeous evening colors.

  ![Sunset over a Hawaiian beach](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?sunset,beach,hawaii)

- **Tour a Kona coffee farm** – walk the orchards, learn about processing, and sample fresh-roasted beans straight from the source.

  ![Coffee farm on a hillside](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?coffee,farm,plantation)

- **Hike Hawaii Volcanoes National Park** – see steaming craters, lava fields, and (conditions permitting) glowing lava at Kilauea, about two hours south of Kona.

  ![Volcanic lava field](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?volcano,lava)

- **Go stargazing or drive up Mauna Kea** – one of the best stargazing spots on Earth thanks to its elevation and clear skies.

  ![Stars over a mountain summit at night](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?stars,night-sky,mountain)

- **Explore Pu'uhonua o Honaunau (Place of Refuge)** – a sacred historical site with restored temples, carved statues, and a peaceful shoreline.

  ![Historic Hawaiian temple site](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?hawaiian,temple,historic)

## Things to Eat & Try

- **Kona coffee** – rich, smooth, and grown nowhere else in the United States; a must for any coffee lover.
- **Poke bowls** – fresh, cubed raw fish (often ahi tuna) marinated and served over rice, a Hawaiian staple found at markets and food trucks everywhere.

  ![Fresh poke bowl with rice and vegetables](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?poke,bowl,food)

- **Shave ice** – finely shaved ice with tropical syrup flavors, a refreshing treat after a day at the beach.
- **Loco moco** – rice topped with a hamburger patty, fried egg, and gravy, the ultimate Hawaiian comfort food.
- **Malasadas** – Portuguese-style fried doughnuts rolled in sugar, a beloved local snack.
- **Fresh tropical fruit** – try lilikoi (passion fruit), rambutan, and locally grown pineapple at roadside stands and farmers markets.

  ![Assorted fresh tropical fruit](https://source.unsplash.com/1600x900/?tropical,fruit)

With its coffee farms, volcanic landscapes, and laid-back coastal towns, Kona is the perfect introduction to the Big Island and to Hawaii as a whole. I can't wait to plan the details and finally see it in person.
