## Must-See Locations

- **Tokyo** - Shibuya Crossing, Shinjuku, Akihabara, Asakusa & Senso-ji Temple, Tokyo Skytree
- **Kyoto** - Fushimi Inari Shrine (thousands of torii gates), Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, Gion district
- **Osaka** - Dotonbori, Osaka Castle, Shinsekai
- **Mount Fuji** - Hakone or the Fuji Five Lakes area for classic views
- **Hiroshima & Miyajima** - Peace Memorial Park, the floating torii gate at Itsukushima Shrine
- **Nara** - Nara Park to see the free-roaming deer, Todai-ji Temple
- **Nikko** - Toshogu Shrine and surrounding nature, a nice day/overnight trip from Tokyo

## Things to Eat

- Sushi and sashimi (Tsukiji Outer Market / Toyosu Market in Tokyo)
- Ramen (try different regional styles - tonkotsu, shoyu, miso)
- Takoyaki and okonomiyaki (Osaka specialties)
- Wagyu beef / yakiniku
- Tempura
- Katsu curry
- Onigiri and other convenience store (konbini) snacks - surprisingly good!
- Matcha desserts (Kyoto is famous for this)
- Izakaya-style small plates with a local beer or sake

## Things to Experience

- Relax in an onsen (hot spring), including ones with a view of Mount Fuji
- Stay at least one night in a traditional ryokan
- Ride the Shinkansen (bullet train) between cities
- Visit a themed cafe (cat cafe, owl cafe, maid cafe in Akihabara)
- Catch cherry blossoms (sakura) if visiting in spring
- Watch a sumo wrestling match or tournament
- Sing karaoke in a private room
- Walk through the torii gate tunnels at Fushimi Inari
- Explore the arcades and electronics stores of Akihabara

## Where to Buy Pokémon Cards

- **Pokémon Center** stores - official shops with exclusive merchandise and cards (locations in Tokyo Skytree, Shibuya, Sunshine City Ikebukuro, Osaka, Kyoto, and more)
- **Pokémon Cafe** (Tokyo & Osaka) - themed cafe that also sells limited merchandise
- **Nintendo TOKYO** in Shibuya Parco
- **Akihabara** - a hub for card shops, including Hareruya, Trader, and Mint, which sell both new packs and rare singles
- **Yodobashi Camera** and **Bic Camera** - large electronics stores with toy/card sections
- **Don Quijote** - discount stores that often stock Pokémon card booster packs
- **Mercari** or **Suruga-ya** - if looking for used, rare, or vintage cards
